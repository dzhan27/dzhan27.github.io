Undergrad Research Assistant @ Seyler Group

Sept 2021 - Present | Ithaca, NY

I'm modifying a simulation to model the magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) flow of plasmas in the presence of magnetic fields by including terms to account for the Hall effect. This has a direct application to various nuclear fusion reactor designs, where fuel must be ignited to plasma-like conditions before fusion reactions can occur. The program is being developed in Fortran 95.

Undergrad Research Assistant @ Fuchs Group

Sept 2021 - Present | Ithaca, NY

I'm developing a program to model the time-based evolution of hexagonal Boron Nitride vacancy centers. These centers are strongly photoluminescent under certain transitions and may allow for accurate quantum sensing applications on the nanometer resolution scale. This program is also being generalized to simulate the well-researched NV center in diamond and verify results of that system found in literature. The program is being developed in Python using the QuTiP (Quantum Toolbox in Python) library.

Physics Lab Technician Intern @ Honeywell

Jun 2021 - Aug 2021 | Broomfield, Colorado

I developed a Surface Ion Trap Chip tester to automate testing of Honeywell Quantumâ€™s surface electrode traps. This program is written in Python and interfaces with NI measurement chasse to measure said traps' electrical characteristics. It includes a live hardware calibration, capacitance and resistance tests, a histogram plotter to visualize data, and a GUI for less software-savvy users. It reduces testing time by over 90%, personnel needed to test the trap by 100% (wow!), and scales linearly with trap size - which is important when Honeywell Quantum makes larger and more sophisticated ion traps.

Software Engineer @ Cornell Mars Rover

Sept 2020 - Present | Ithaca, New York

My team of ~70 student engineers builds a rover from scratch every year to compete in an annual University Rover Challenge. It encompasses a multitude of tasks a rover on Mars would perform, including autonomous navigation, analysis of soil samples for signs of life, and communication with a remote controller. I implemented Inverse Kinematics for the Rover's arm to enable intelligent, automated endpoint movement without requiring specification for individual motor writes and angles, thereby improving Rover competition prospects. I also defined macro movement sequences in C++ for the arm to collect and deposit soil to be analyzed at an optimal approach and orientation.
